Most gas plan comparison sites show you the per-therm rate and call it a day. That number alone is almost meaningless.

Here's a small example. Two plans:

- **Plan A:** $0.59 / therm, $5.95 monthly customer charge
- **Plan B:** $0.79 / therm, no monthly charge

At 30 therms a month — typical summer usage in the Southeast — Plan A costs $23.65, Plan B costs $23.70. Effectively identical.

At 120 therms — typical winter — Plan A costs $76.75, Plan B costs $94.80. Plan A wins by $18.

Now flip the usage profile. If you barely use gas (maybe 10 therms in summer because you only have a gas water heater), Plan B wins by nearly $5 every month. Over a low-usage summer, that's most of the year.

## What the analyzer does

It runs your actual 12-month usage against every available plan in your state, including:

- Per-therm rate (with tier breakpoints where applicable)
- Monthly fixed customer charge
- Promotional first-month rates
- Distribution / delivery fees that pass through unchanged

Then it sorts by total annual cost. No marketing. No "lowest rate" puffery.

## Why bother

Natural gas plans churn. Promotional rates expire. New providers enter the market. The plan that was best for you two years ago probably isn't best today, and the gap can be $100–$300 a year for moderate to heavy users.

Worth ten minutes once a year.
